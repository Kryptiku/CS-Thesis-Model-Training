Conclusions

Primary Finding (A2C)

The source of randomness (PRNG vs QRNG) does not produce statistically significant differences in maze structural properties. Both A2C agents show comparable evaluation performance, consistent with PPO results.

  • Maze Structure: Identical to PPO analysis — all 9 structural metrics showed identical medians. Mann-Whitney U tests confirmed no significant difference (0/9 metrics, all p > 0.05).
  • Agent Performance: The PRNG-trained A2C agent achieved 55% intra / 53% cross success rate; the QRNG-trained A2C agent achieved 57% intra / 61% cross. Both agents performed comparably with no clear advantage from either randomness source.
  • Generalization: The PRNG agent showed a 2% generalization gap, while the QRNG agent showed a negative gap (-4%), meaning it performed slightly better on cross-distribution mazes. Neither gap is practically meaningful.
  • Training Convergence: The QRNG agent reached a higher final success rate (87% vs 65%) despite seeing far fewer unique mazes (144 vs 2,988). This suggests the QRNG agent may have overtrained on its smaller maze set, achieving high training performance but moderate evaluation performance.
  • A2C vs PPO: A2C agents achieved lower evaluation success rates (53-61%) compared to PPO agents (68-79%), consistent with PPO's known advantage in sample efficiency for complex tasks. However, both algorithms yield the same conclusion: quantum randomness does not confer a measurable advantage.
