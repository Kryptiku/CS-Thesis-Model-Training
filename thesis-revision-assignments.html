Member 1 — Front Matter & Foundations

Section Revision Task
Abstract Simplify sentences; reduce technical overload; keep key results clear and direct.
Background Add a clear research gap statement at the end of the section:

“This study addresses the lack of empirical evaluation of QRNG-based randomness in procedural maze generation and its impact on reinforcement learning generalization.”
Statement of the Problem Make questions concise. Ensure each question is: clear, measurable, and aligned to results.
Objectives Rewrite into the following four measurable objectives:
  1. Compare QRNG- and PRNG-based maze generation in terms of entropy, randomness, and structural diversity.
  2. Evaluate DRL agent performance under QRNG- and PRNG-generated environments using success rate and generalization metrics.
  3. Analyze the impact of randomness type on learning stability and policy robustness.
  4. Validate observed differences using statistical testing.
Novelty Add a novel contribution framing sentence:

“This study introduces a novel experimental evaluation of quantum-derived randomness in procedural environment generation and its effect on reinforcement learning generalization.”
Significance Refocus significance on: procedural generation, DRL training environments, and randomness quality. Remove overly broad claims about AI, society, and research in general.
